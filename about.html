What make us unique?

Whether you're planning a trip to Portugal or Brazil or simply eager to enhance your Portuguese language skills, we're here to guide you every step of the way. Our experienced teachers, all certified in Portuguese as a Foreign Language (PTFL Certification), are fluent in Portuguese and possess a deep understanding of both Portuguese and Brazilian cultures. All of our instructors have lived and worked in Portugal and Brazil, offering firsthand insights and captivating stories to enrich your learning experience.

What about skill Levels?

European and Brazilian Portuguese possess distinct accents, and our classes cater to learners of all levels in both dialects. We specialize in guiding absolute beginners through their language journey. Students can initially focus on either European or Brazilian Portuguese to suit their preferences. Our comprehensive approach empowers you to speak, read, write, and think fluently in Portuguese. Whether you're a seasoned language learner or just starting out, we're dedicated to supporting your linguistic and cultural exploration.

Levels of classes we offer:

  • Beginner
  • Intermediate
  • Advanced
  • Conversation Group
  • Cultural Workshop

We encourage all of our students to practice the language in-between classes with Duolingo.

Did you know?

The Brazilian flag is also rich in symbolism, representing the country's history, geography, and aspirations.

  • The green and yellow colors symbolize the House of Braganza (green) and the House of Habsburg (yellow), the families of Brazil's first emperor and empress, respectively. They also represent the vast tropical forests and the country's natural wealth.
  • The white band with "Ordem e Progresso" (Order and Progress): This motto, inspired by Auguste Comte's Positivism, reflects the nation's desire for progress and stability.
  • The blue circle represents the sky over Rio de Janeiro on the day of the proclamation of the republic.
  • The 27 stars represent the 26 states and the Federal District. Their positions correspond to the celestial map of Rio de Janeiro on November 15, 1889.
  • Southern Cross constellation: This prominent constellation in the Southern Hemisphere signifies Brazil's location.
